Ghibli Character Encyclopedia

A tribute to the most inspiring and meaningful characters from Studio Ghibli films.

Spirited Away Characters

Chihiro Ogino

Chihiro starts as a timid girl who finds herself trapped in the spirit world. Through grit and kindness, she matures into a brave and selfless heroine.

Lesson: Courage and growth come from facing your fears, not avoiding them.

Haku

Haku is a mysterious spirit who helps Chihiro navigate the world of the bathhouse, while struggling with his forgotten identity.

Lesson: Knowing your true name and identity is powerful—never forget who you are.

No-Face

No-Face starts as a quiet spirit but becomes greedy and chaotic when exposed to the bathhouse’s greed. He later finds peace with Zeniba.

Lesson: Environment shapes behavior—kindness and calm bring out the best in others.

Earwig and the Witch Characters

Earwig

A clever and resourceful orphan girl who finds herself adopted by a witch, learning to navigate magic and manipulation.

Lesson: Quick thinking and adaptability help overcome difficult situations.

Witch Bella Yaga

The strict and demanding witch who adopts Earwig, expecting obedience and magical prowess.

Lesson: Power can be harsh, but understanding others’ motives leads to cooperation.

Thomas

A kind-hearted but bumbling apprentice who assists Bella Yaga and sometimes struggles to keep up with Earwig’s wit.

Lesson: Kindness and persistence are valuable even when you’re not the smartest.

My Neighbor Totoro Characters

Totoro

A forest spirit who befriends Satsuki and Mei. Though he doesn’t speak, Totoro provides comfort, wonder, and protection.

Lesson: Nature is magical and healing — we should respect and cherish it.

Satsuki Kusakabe

The older sister who cares deeply for Mei and supports her family during tough times.

Lesson: Strength doesn’t always mean being fearless — it’s about showing love when things are hard.

Mei Kusakabe

The curious and brave younger sister who discovers Totoro’s forest world.

Lesson: Wonder and imagination help us cope with uncertainty and change.

Princess Mononoke Characters

Ashitaka

A kind and brave prince cursed by a demon. He seeks peace between humans and nature without choosing sides.

Lesson: Empathy and understanding can bridge even the deepest divides.

San (Princess Mononoke)

A fierce girl raised by wolves who defends the forest from humans. She struggles with her identity and hatred for mankind.

Lesson: You can fight for what you love without losing who you are.

Lady Eboshi

The leader of Iron Town, strong-willed and industrial. Though she harms nature, she also cares deeply for her people.

Lesson: People are complex — good and bad can exist in the same heart.

Ponyo Characters

Ponyo

A goldfish princess who longs to become human. Her curiosity and love bring magic and joy to the world above the sea.

Lesson: Innocence and love can change even the biggest obstacles.

Sousuke

A caring and brave boy who befriends Ponyo. His loyalty and kindness help bridge two very different worlds.

Lesson: Friendship and trust can overcome fear and uncertainty.

Fujimoto

Ponyo’s father, a powerful but protective wizard of the sea. He struggles with letting Ponyo go but ultimately wants her happiness.

Lesson: Letting go is sometimes the greatest act of love.

Howl's Moving Castle Characters

Howl

A mysterious wizard with a moving castle. Despite his flamboyance and fear of conflict, he shows deep compassion and bravery.

Lesson: True strength is found in protecting others, not avoiding battles.

Sophie

A humble hatmaker who is cursed into an old woman. Her journey reveals her inner strength, confidence, and capacity for love.

Lesson: Beauty and worth come from within, not appearances.

Calcifer

A fire demon bound to Howl’s heart. While snarky and proud, he’s deeply loyal and helps power the moving castle.

Lesson: Loyalty and heart can fuel even the most magical transformations.

Castle in the Sky Characters

Sheeta

A girl with a mysterious crystal who holds the secret to the legendary floating island, Laputa. She is brave, kind, and resourceful.

Lesson: Kindness and courage can guide you through the darkest mysteries.

Pazu

An orphaned boy who dreams of discovering Laputa. His determination and loyalty to Sheeta help them uncover the truth together.

Lesson: Never give up on your dreams, especially when they can change the world.

Laputan Robot

A silent guardian of Laputa, the robot shows both the destructive power and the gentle protectiveness of ancient technology.

Lesson: True strength lies in choosing to protect, not destroy.

Kiki’s Delivery Service Characters

Kiki

A young witch-in-training who starts a delivery service in a new town. Her journey is about independence, self-discovery, and resilience.

Lesson: Believing in yourself is the first step to finding your place in the world.

Jiji

Kiki's loyal black cat companion. Sarcastic, witty, and always by her side, Jiji brings humor and wisdom to Kiki’s journey.

Lesson: Support and friendship can make even the scariest days manageable.

Tombo

A quirky and curious boy who admires Kiki's flying abilities. His genuine interest and encouragement help Kiki find confidence.

Lesson: Sometimes, the people who believe in you most are the ones you least expect.

The Tale of the Princess Kaguya Characters

Princess Kaguya

A celestial being born from a bamboo stalk. Her life is filled with beauty, sorrow, and a longing for freedom and truth.

Lesson: True happiness comes from living authentically and embracing life’s joys and pains.

Sutemaru

Kaguya’s childhood friend who represents a simple and joyful life close to nature. Their bond is one of innocence and deep affection.

Lesson: Sometimes the most meaningful connections are found in the simplest moments.

Okina (Miyatsuko)

The bamboo cutter who raises Kaguya as his daughter. Though loving, he is blinded by ambition, which leads to her sorrow.

Lesson: Parental love must be guided by understanding, not control or pride.
